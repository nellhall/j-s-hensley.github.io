About

I am currently a second year Ph.D. student in the Electrical Engineering department at Stanford University, working in information theory and inference. I am advised by Mert Pilanci.

Before arriving at Stanford, I did my undergraduate and M.Eng. degree at MIT in Electrical Engineering and Computer Science. While there, I worked with Thomas Heldt on reducing false alarm rates in the intensive care unit. My thesis, Characterization of monitoring alarms in a community hospital intensive care unit, can be found here.

I also worked in the Robot Locomotion Group on Valkyrie, a humanoid robot from NASA, and won Best Research Presentation in the senior research capstone class for my work on it.

You can reach me at hensleys at stanford.edu. You can also find my GitHub here. The design of this website was created by Eleanor Hall.
